How the learning works

Three modes, with training wheels that come off one at a time.

Mimic — today's live game: click and key-press through real Blender workflows on screenshots, with spoken instructions and a robot guide who stands beside every target. Guided — the same quest inside real Blender, with a companion addon verifying each step against the actual scene. Free build — just the brief, checked at the end.

In Guided mode, assistance is layers the student toggles, and finishing with fewer layers on earns more stars:

  • Highlights — the addon points at the next button
  • Ghost — a translucent target model in the viewport
  • Narration — spoken step-by-step instructions

A platform, not an app

Accounts, classes, and motivation systems that reward real skill.

Kid-safe accounts

Class code + picture password — no student email, no PII. Teachers and volunteer mentors get a progress heatmap of the whole class.

Badges that mean something

Named after real Blender skills — earned across quests by using the actual tools, not by logging in.

The robot is the reward

New dances, expressions, and cosmetics for each student's guide robot — kids grind for their companion, not for a number.

Teams and publishing

Quests support team runs, and every completion offers a one-click share video — the portfolio builds itself.

A companion in real Blender

The addon guides, then verifies: active object, selection, modifier stack, operator history. Progress that can't be faked.

Content records itself

The same addon captures an instructor modeling session — screenshots, inputs, operators — and turns it into a new quest automatically.

The farm, then the garden

Healthy competition, with a living payoff.

The virtual farm runs a real regenerative loop — food waste feeds black soldier flies, flies feed fish, fish and beets make fertilizer — and student robots tend every stage. Competition is about yield, energy, and reliability under a parts budget, so clever design beats brute force. The simulation is a true digital twin: the same telemetry schema as the physical garden.

Graduated designs run on a standardized chassis fleet in a real indoor botanical garden. Students log in daily to a live view of their plot, telemetry, and a time-lapse of what their robot is growing — and queue tomorrow's tweaks through a stewarded safety envelope. Plants grow on plant time: a living thing depends on your engineering.
